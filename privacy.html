Pitchside Lineup

Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 2026

Pitchside Lineup is a fully offline app. We do not collect, store, or transmit any of your personal data. Everything stays on your device.

What Data We Collect

None. Pitchside Lineup does not collect any personal information, usage data, analytics, or telemetry. The app does not connect to the internet and has no server-side component.

What Data Is Stored on Your Device

The app stores the following data locally on your device only:

  • Team names and player names you enter
  • Lineup configurations and formations
  • Match scores and statistics
  • Your app preferences and settings (kit colours, sport type, etc.)

This data never leaves your device. It is stored using your device's local storage and is not accessible to us or any third party.

Third-Party Services

Pitchside Lineup does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking services. No SDKs or libraries in the app transmit data externally.

Sharing Features

The app includes a feature to share lineup images. When you use this feature, the image is shared using your device's native share sheet (e.g., WhatsApp, email, AirDrop). We do not process, intercept, or store any shared content. The sharing is handled entirely by your device's operating system.

Children's Privacy

Pitchside Lineup does not collect any data from any user, including children. The app is suitable for all ages as it contains no data collection, no advertising, no in-app purchases, and no online features.

Data Deletion

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can delete all app data at any time by:

  • Using the app's built-in data management options
  • Clearing the app's data through your device settings
  • Uninstalling the app (this removes all stored data)

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, we will post the revised version on this page with an updated effective date. As the app is offline, changes to this policy will not affect how the app handles your data — because it doesn't handle any.
